Clarinetist Hattie Bestul strives to inspire passion, kindness, and courage in others through music. Her focus is making music accessible to people of all circumstances through performance and education. Hattie's desire to share music's benefits compelled her to develop and teach an exploratory music course, "Beyond the Notes," at the University of Wisconsin - Madison College for Kids program and to give creative music presentations in middle and high school music classrooms. Most recently Hattie returned as a guest artist with an interactive presentation to the rural Wisconsin, Kickapoo Valley Area school where she first began playing clarinet. In addition to working with youth, Hattie is actively designing innovative concert experiences tailored to give all audiences a greater personal connection to music. To this end, Hattie has given recitals that included audience engagement through interactive teaching artistry and the premier performances of modern composers' works.

Though she is a Colorado native, Hattie grew up in Wisconsin and now calls the Madison area home. It was her time performing in the Wisconsin Youth Symphony Orchestra that set the stage for her pursuit of music as a career. As an undergraduate at the University of Nebraska - Lincoln Glenn Korff School of Music, Hattie's most treasured experiences include: performing Brahms's Clarinet Quintet as a guest artist with the Chiara String Quartet, soloing with the UNL Wind Ensemble, and performing as a member of the Laurel Trio (a cello, clarinet, piano chamber ensemble). Having completed a Business Minor along with a Bachelor degree in Music Performance at UNL with Dr. Diane Barger, Hattie has continued her studies at the Eastman School of Music in the clarinet studio of Ken Grant and currently resides in Rochester, NY.

Continually refining her focus on avenues for community arts programs, teaching artistry, and performance innovations, Hattie was accepted into Eastman's Arts Leadership Program (ALP) in August 2016. As an ALP Fellow, Hattie is excited to take on internships in Development and Operations with ROCmusic Collaborative in the Fall 2017 as well as in Development and Marketing for the Eastman Community Music School in the Spring of 2018. Hattie will graduate in May 2018 with an Arts Leadership Certificate as well as a Masters in Music in Performance and Literature. Following completion of this degree, Hattie plans to launch her career as a performer and teaching artist in order to expand art and music's impact on communities.

Wisconsin Roots
In May 2017 Hattie returned to her clarinet roots in rural Wisconsin as a guest artist at the Kickapoo Area School District middle and high school band classes. She worked with students through an interactive presentation and performance where they considered patterns and their context in music and experimented with expression in cases of repetition.
Beyond the Notes
In Summer 2015 Hattie developed and taught "Beyond the Notes" an exploratory music course for rising 6th graders in the last year of UW-Madison's College for Kids Program. Daily clarinet performances by Hattie were paired with corresponding creative projects. The class covered basic musical concepts, physics of sound waves, the human ear, music history, and performance anxiety.
Imaginings: A Recital of New Works for Clarinet -- April 2015
Hattie held a recital featuring three world premieres by three UNL students/alumni on April 26, 2015. The program centered on Daniel Baldwin’s "Imaginings," a work composed for Hattie Bestul and inspired by a poem by Sallie Bestul. Also featured were Rachel Whelan’s Sonata for Clarinet, and Jared Brinkman’s Night Thoughts.
Music for Everyone Initiative
The Music for Everyone Initiative aimed to invite community -- particularly young musicians -- to UNL student recitals during Fall 2014. Through teaching artist presentations and a web sign-up, over 200 people received student recital notifications, and Hattie’s audience-interactive junior recital titled Music for Everyone launched the initiative in October 2014.
